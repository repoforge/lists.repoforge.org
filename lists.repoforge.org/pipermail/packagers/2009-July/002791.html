Also the nodist (or 0-tagged) packages have a special meaning. They are 
distribution agnostic and for that reason they appear in all repositories.

The reason for this is that some games or other packages include data 
files that are architecture-independent (like sound/video/images). For 
that reason we package them seperately and hardlink those packages cross 
distribution repository.

Everything else should definitely be looked at, however some of the 
packages simply don't build anymore (and used to build). So removing them 
is not a good option, leaving them doesn't hurt but doesn't look good.
