On Nov 30, 2009, at 8:33 AM, Karanbir Singh wrote:

> None, spam filtering is a client issue :)


thanks for clearing that up for me :)  there's very rarely a spam from  
the svn-commits list that makes it to my inbox, so that's not a  
problem for me, but unfortunately it does mean that the web archive of  
svn-commits is almost entirely unusable.

this guy's page (http://www.arschkrebs.de/postfix/postfix_restriction_classes.shtml 
) leads me to believe that you might be able to fix the problem with  
something like this:

in /etc/postfix/main.cf:

smtpd_delay_reject = yes
smtpd_recipient_restrictions = hash:/etc/postfix/recipients,  
permit_mynetworks, reject_unauth_destination

smtpd_restriction_classes = svn-commits_restrictions
svn-commits_restrictions = check_sender_access hash:/etc/postfix/ 
rpmforge-trusted

in /etc/postfix/recipients:

svn-commits@	svn-commits_restrictions

in /etc/postfix/recipients:

<ip.address.of.svnhost>	OK

run `postmap /etc/postfix/recipients /etc/postfix/rpmforge-trusted`  
and then restart postfix.

this didn't work for me in my initial testing here; perhaps someone  
who is more familiar with postfix than i can see what the problem is?
