On Thu, 2011-02-17 at 22:50 +0600, Denis Fateyev wrote:

> The question is, what I have to do, if I want to update it to the
> latest available version (20090401): shall I follow the existing
> scheme (with "0.0" as the version and "real_version" in additional),
> or I have to change spec like in ntfs-3g example above?

Well, unless you have a compelling reason to not doing so, I personally
would suggest you to stick to the way we used to do it with
0.%{real_version}. In the end, it should not really hurt your eyes all
that much :-)

The numbering tends to change from one day to another (i.e. ntfs-3g was
not always following the date-based numbering, look at what kind of mess
is ffmpeg / mplayer, now Ubuntu-style YY.MM numbering is becoming trendy
etc.) and it will be a real PITA to bump the epoch & deal with the
resulting problems later.

IMHO.
