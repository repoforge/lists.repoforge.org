+<?_head('About RPMforge.net')?>
+
+<?_title('Once upon a time...')?>
+In late 2000, Matthias Saou started building quite a few RPM packages
+for his own needs, that couldn't easily be found elsewhere, or at least
+not so well suited for Red Hat Linux. A few months later, the website on
+which these packages were hosted changed its name to FreshRPMS.net.
+<p>
+
+Inspired by the work of Matthias, other packagers appeared, like Axel Thimm,
+Rudolf Kaztl, Fernando Lopez-Lezcano, Dag Wieers and Dries Verachtert. This
+new situation lead to various incompatibilities, although most repositories
+stayed true and compatible with FreshRPMS.
+
+<?_title('Troubling changes')?>
+At the end of 2002, the fedora.us project was initiated to streamline those
+efforts and was initially endorsed by most of the existing packagers, though,
+after some months of endless discussions 2 camps formed. On one side you had
+the existing packagers and on the other the fedora.us maintainers.
+<p>
+
+While the existing repositories had a huge collection of packages and
+fedora.us had only few, fedora.us insisted to become the *one and only*
+place for packages, whereas existing packagers were more concerned about
+how to work together and have the infrastructure in place to colaborate.
+<p>
+
+Mid 2003, fedora.us was elected by Red Hat to become the community initiative
+for their Fedora community project, which left the existing packagers in the
+cold. The existing packagers, lead by Matthias Saou, slowly formed
+RPMforge.net.
+
+<?_title('Important differences')?>
+RPMforge.net and fedora.us differ in a few important ways:
+<ol>
+  <li> RPMforge.net is independent and not endorsed by Red Hat
+  <li> RPMforge.net is also packaging for Red Hat Enterprise Linux
+  <li> RPMforge.net is open to work together with other projects
+  <ol type="a">
+    <li> together with SuSE packagers
+    <li> with Red Hat Enterprise rebuild projects like TaoLinux and CentOS
+    <li> with Red Hat based distributions like BLAG and cAos
+    <li> with Red Hat based distributions on other architectures like Aurora Linux and Yellow Dog Linux
+  </ol>
+  <li> RPMforge.net is open to work with other packagers and repository towards compatibility
+  <li> The community will decide how RPMforge will advance
+</ol>
+
+
+<?_foot()?>
