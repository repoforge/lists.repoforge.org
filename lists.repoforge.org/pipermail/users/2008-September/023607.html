Peter, can I urge you to bundle your Perl modules as RPM's wherever 
feasible, for your safety and ours? Dag and Dries tools for building 
RPMforge packages are workable, although I find them requiring a bit of 
extra work to deal with their built-in support for multi-platform 
compilation. If not, the 'cpan2rpm' package is pretty cool and pretty 
useful. This gets your modules under source control management, and 
helps avoid overwriting your system files or grabbing one bit of a 
module from one location, finding another incompatible bit from another 
location, etc.

The reporting of conflicting versions of the same tool is pretty 
important, especially when you start updating modules that are built 
into the base Perl package by RedHat or that migratte into or out of the 
base Perl package with different releases.

And mind you, I think some competent people from RedHat read this list, 
to get just these sorts of warnings and let people know they're an issue.
