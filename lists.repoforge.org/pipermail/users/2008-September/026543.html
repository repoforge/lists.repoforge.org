Yes, the error below is reasonably clear, though you have to read carefully.

That file is provided by two rpms of different names from different
repositories.

You have libmad from rpmforge, and libmad0 (likely) from epel. Since
they have different names, they are treated as different packages, but
they provide the same file.

Mixing repositories like rpmforge and epel will occasionally produce
results like this, and you have to be careful to update your packages
from the repo you start with. Mixing and matching can lead to rather
complex issues down the road. See if this explanation makes sense
again when you look at the error below.
