Dag Wieers wrote:

> I am wondering if we should provide a seperate repository with packages
> that break with existing standards. Or even the packages that replace
> upstream packages.
> 
> In that case your subversion EL4 stuff would be no problem to me and
> people can simply opt out. For some time I have resisted it, but maybe I
> have to accept I am wrong and at least allow these packages to be
> available.
> 
> What do others think ? Should we put this on the list of things to foresee
> in the future ?

Trying to deal with modern perl modules while EL packages multiple outdated
highly used dependencies is a constant annoyance.  Our current solution is
to build our own packages to override the outdated base packages along with
the modules that rpmforge can not build due to reliance on EL packages.

I have a feeling this is a very common situation.  Another route others
could go is to just bring everything in house and make all packages custom
built, which has recently crossed my mind.

It seems like having a repo managed in the rpmforge manner with this one
policy change would allow those that don't have the knowledge, time or other
resources to still have access to the software they need.

Providing such a repo, at least on an experimental bases initially might be
an interesting test.
