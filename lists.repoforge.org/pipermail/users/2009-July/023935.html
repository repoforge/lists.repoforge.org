On Wed, 1 Jul 2009, Philip Durbin wrote:

> Hello, I just installed perl-Mail-Box and a dependency, 
> perl-Object-Realize-Later, is not captured in the RPM:
>
> [root at zmb-1 ~]# rpm -q --requires perl-Mail-Box | grep Realize
> [root at zmb-1 ~]#
>
> I'm curious why the perl-Object-Realize-Later dependency was not included in 
> the perl-Mail-Box RPM, as I believe the author of the Perl module specified 
> it correctly.
>
> [root at zmb-1 ~]# grep -E "requires|Realize" 
> /usr/share/doc/perl-Mail-Box-2.084/META.yml
> requires:
>     Object::Realize::Later:        0.14
>
> Should a bug be filed upstream?  Or is something broken in the process 
> (DAR??) that adds requirements to RPMs?  I don't see anything in the wiki 
> about this.

Good question. We do have SPEC files that still date back from before we 
used an automated process. I just checked what our tool would do 
(dar-diff-perl.sh) and it would in fact remove the BuildRequires 
altogether. So it is safe to assume that our parser doesn't do this 
correctly.

At LinuxTag Christoph told me we should rework our perl-management tools 
in perl because all the functionality is available from modules. And I 
agree :)
