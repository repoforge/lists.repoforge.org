Why Release: 0%{?dist} and not 1%{?dist} ?

When I build them myself, I always number them 0 so that when they
come out in RPMforge or EPEL, I update to the upstream version. I'm
happy to submit them to RPMforge with:

> Release: 1%{?dist}

instead, it just takes a bit of editing when I do the submission.
Right now, I'm chewing through the components of FW-tools, which is a
package that *really* irritates me. Its author simply publishes an
insertable binary blob. Since all its components are open source if
not freeware, there's little excuse for not making the build setup
available, and people can wind up installing the bundle to get one or
two tools that are *already* available for their distribution, and not
realize that they're available or that bugs have been fixed in one
place, not the other.

I've gotten all the comopnents except 'openev', which has dependencies
on 'gtkglarea' which hasn't been included since CentOS 3.9. I do enjoy
cleaning up after developers who just grab and plug components: it
helps pay my wages.
