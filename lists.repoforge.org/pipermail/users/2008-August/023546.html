> First is the fact that the rules updating program (sa-update) that comes

I don't have time to go through the setup and answer you properly here, but
that isn't true. If you understood how the rules work and get applied, you'll
realise there's nothing wrong with the sa-update script.

> Secondly, since users and administrators alike are totally unaware 
> of the fact mentioned above, there are  floating around some 
> "tutorials" for installing spamassassin, in which it is recommended 
> to upgrade to surforge's spamassassing version, along with an auto-
> updating program such as "rules dujour" :). Now to complicate 
> matters even further "rules dujour" utilizes rulesets provided by 
> SARE, and sre installed in directory"/etc/mail/spamassassin", thus 
> leading to the creation of a TRIPLICATE set of of rules.

If you've been around the traps for a while, you'll realise that the
rulesdujour script is deprecated and mustn't be used. It was actually
announced over 18 months ago now. If you check the sare mailing list archives,
you'll see what I'm talking about.
