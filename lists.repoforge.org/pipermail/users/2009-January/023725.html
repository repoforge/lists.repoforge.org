On Mon, 19 Jan 2009, Ville Mattila wrote:

> Please consider applying the attached patch to nagios.spec against
> the current revision 6654.  It adds %config(noreplace) protection for
> Nagios HTML directory tree %{_datadir}/nagios/.  This is to prevent RPM
> upgrades from overwriting local modifications to HTML stylesheets etc.
> (Currently I need to modify stylesheets/status.css every time Nagios
> gets an RPM upgrade to stop Status Summaries from blinking).

Hey Ville,

I do understand your need, but we have to make sure that a change like 
this accomodates the majority of our users. I am not convinced that this 
change accomodates the majority of our users though.

If you think your stylesheet fix is an improvement over the default, it 
might be better to have your fix applied to Nagios. In general I have less 
problems marking files in /etc with 'noreplace' (like we do with 
initscripts), but for files people in general should not have to modify, I 
prefer not to mark them 'noreplace'.

Unless other people send in compelling reasons or there is a widespread 
need to modify CSS or HTML files, I would advise not to change this.

PS In your case, there are other solutions that would prevent RPM to 
replace the files you are using. Moving it to another directory would be 
one.
