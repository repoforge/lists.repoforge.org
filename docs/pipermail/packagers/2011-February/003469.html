> Other than the mock suggestion by Erik, what other tools are helpful in the
> backporting process?
>


Mock is only the tool for building packages, not for backporting process
itself.
You should modify X.Org sources, write some patches needed for compatibility
with RHEL kernel.
Building package (with mock or without) will be your last and easiest stage.


The tools are the compiler, your favorite text editor (IDE, optionally) and
some knowledge about the kernel internal architecture in the part, which
provides interaction with X.Org. RedHat official backporters may give you
some additional details about it via their technical mailing lists. But, I
believe they will not advise you to backport X.Org due to a lot of changes
that leads to additional stability testing in distrib, but generally has an
uncertain benefit.
