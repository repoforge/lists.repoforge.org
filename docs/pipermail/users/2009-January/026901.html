> Also just out of personal interest why did you choose apt-get over the
> "oficially supported" yum? Is there any advantage?
> This is not meant to be negative about apt use i am just curious.
>   

yum is not the package manager of first choice for other distros - I 
have mostly Debian-based systems (e.g., Ubuntu and "pure" Debian) here, 
and I simply have a little more experience with it.  If I were only 
using RHEL/CentOS/Fedora, I'd not be concerned with apt for any other 
reason than there are many who say it is a "better" way to manage updates.

But there are many who prefer chocolate to vanilla, or vice versa, while 
still others who like them both and see no compelling need to choose on 
over the other.  I have been lucky and never had an "apt-get" on a 
Debian system break my computer (those "two pieces" that worry most OSS 
users), while I have had rpm do damage on an otherwise "correctly 
maintained" RHEL system.  My experience with yum is insufficient for me 
to comment on its reliability and general robustness.
