Simon J. Mudd does a fine job of providing a postfix src.rpm with 
various options. He is having server issues at the moment, and i'll 
forward his latest on that shortly to you.

Info on his mailing list can be found at: 
http://lists.wl0.org/mailman/listinfo/postfix-rpm-announce

With the large # of build options available postfix other than the 
default build does not lend itself to an automated build process below 
are links to the rpm/src I use which is built with mysql, tls and sasld 
support.

http://www.aidant.org/mrepo/aidant-i386/RPMS.el5/postfix-2.4.5-3.pcre.mysql.sasl2.el5.ae.i386.rpm
